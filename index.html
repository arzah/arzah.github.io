What is Rococo?

Rococo is an art movement that took place in the late 17th century and is known for being very extravagant and ornamental.

The Birth of Rococo

The Rococo movement began in France 1699 after the then French king, Louis XIV demanded for more youthful work to be created under his reign. During this time Jeane Antoine Watteau could be considered the “father” of the Rococo movement, giving birth to a new technique called “fêtes galantes” and his fluid artwork served as influence to many others.
